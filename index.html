Catherine E. Majors, PhD

NRSA Postdoctoral Fellow
Northwestern University
Evanston, IL

I am a Kirschstein-NRSA Postdoctoral Fellow in the Department of Chemical & Biological Engineering at Northwestern University.

I work in Dr. Keith Tyo's group to engineer recombinant proteins in order to develop an in vitro, self-amplifying protein signaling network for ultrasensitive protein biomarker sensing.

Recent Updates

June 2023: I GOT THE K99/R00!!! I am SO EXCITED for the opportunity to continue my postdoctoral project at Northwestern and finish my project aims before transitioning to a faculty position next year!

May 2023: I had a fun time sitting on the Center for Engineering Sustainability and Resilience's Career Panel! Thanks for inviting me to take part!

April 2023: I was incredibly honored to receive the McBride Postdoctoral Trainee Award this year for my service to NU's postdoctoral community! The awards reception by The Graduate School was lovely, and I enjoyed hearing about all the amazing scholarship and service going on within the TGS community here at NU.

April 2023: That's a wrap on the faculty search market 2022-2023 addition! I had a great time visiting so many amazing schools and programs, but I'd be lying if I said I wasn't relieved it was over so I can rest. Stay tuned to find out where I'm heading as a faculty member!

January 2023: Just got home from Dakar, Senegal after attending the C-THAN Fifth Annual Symposium! It was great to meet and interact with so many amazing engineers and clinicians from around the globe working on point-of-care technologies, and I loved being back in Dakar for the first time in many years!

November 2022: My first AIChE Annual Meeting is in the books! Enjoyed presenting my vision for my future research program and meeting with so many amazing faculty from around the country! I have to be honest: AIChE's reception game is high.

November 2022: I had an amazing time at the Baxter Young Investigators Awards program! I had the opportunity to present my research as well as to learn about the amazing work Baxter is doing in the critical care space.

October 2022: BMES 2022 is a wrap! Loved presenting three different posters on both the experimental and computational aspects of my postdoc project as well as on my vision for a future independent research program.

October 2022: K99/R00 application is SUBMITTED! *fingers crossed*

September 2022: Big thanks to the amazing faculty at MIT ChemE for hosting the Rising Stars Program! I learned so much from faculty and participants alike, and I had an amazing time on MIT's campus in Boston!

September 2022: I was selected as a Baxter Young Investigators Award First-Tier Winner; I'm incredibly honored to get this award, and I'm excited to share my work with the scientists at Baxter in November!

August 2022: My Late Breaker abstract and Meet the Faculty Candidates application were both accepted for the BMES Annual Meeting. Excited to share THREE different presentations in San Antonio in October!

July 2022: My paper was accepted for the Meet the Faculty Candidates Poster Session at AIChE this year. Pretty nervous, but also excited to interface with a new professional society in Phoenix in November!

July 2022: I was accepted to the MIT Chemical Engineering Rising Stars Workshop in Boston in September! I can't wait to meet other female stars in ChemE and learn more about creating a successful faculty application packet and career!

July 2022: My (first) poster for BMES was accepted -- looking forward to seeing everyone in San Antonio in the fall!

July 2022: I loved presenting my work at the Context, Connections, and Community (C3) Symposium. It was an honor to be invited to speak among such an impressive line-up of speakers.

June 2022: So very proud of Unsun Lee -- the absolutely AMAZING undergrad I have had the privilege of working with over the past two years -- as she graduates Northwestern! I can't wait to see all the great work she will continue to do as she moves on to her next adventure doing a post-Bacc at the National Cancer Institute in the fall!

It was great getting to meet the other PDA leadership within the Big10+ PDA that we've been virtually working with over the past few years and to gain new ideas and perspectives for NUPA's continued success!
